Crime and Parks: Data Science Class Project

Overview

Role/Tools

I participated in the search for viable datasets as well as in the coding of the project, cleaning up the initial data and finding ways to visualize the data for correlational purposes.
Tools: Python, JupyterNotebook, pandas library

Problem

Looking at previous studies on the internet, we discovered that there are two main stances on crime and park correlations. On the one hand, crime has been seen as having been correlated with an increase of the number of parks and vegetation nearby. On the other, an increase in park visitors is also correlated by more violent crimes in other studies.

Solution

We used public datasets from San Francisco about the crimes in the past years, park data in the city as well as each park's score. After cleaning the datasets and pulling out the common link, location, we overlayed the park locations on top of the crime locations as a scatterplot. We also attempted to use hexbin plotting before switching over to the Nearest Neighbor method. The number of crimes was still too much so we ended up breaking down the crimes by neighborhood as well as parks. Ultimately, we did not find a correlation between the two.
