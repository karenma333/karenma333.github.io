Retirement Wizard

Team size: 3
Timeline: 3 months (2019)
Role: Research & design

Context

Summary

The existing product was really good for the B2B market and we wanted to shift the target audience to a Direct to consumer model.

Product Opportunity

We needed to adjust our product to be more friendly and diagnosable to account for different levels of financial expertise and less patience.

Initial Research

Methods

I conducted a card sort, some analytics, usability tests and A/B tests to identify what to change.

  • bullet
  • bullet2

Synthesis

From our research we identified two key pain points.

  • Users were uninterested in enrolling because our flow took too long. Our time to value was too long.
  • Users were not interested in our advice or recommendations.

Design Solution

Improved Onboarding

The onboarding flow was updated to provide users context on how many questions were involved, as well as decrease the number of questions asked upfront.

  • bullet
  • bullet2

Improved Recommendations

The product information architecture was updated to better display the information in a manner that users would understand.

  • bullet
  • bullet2

Results

While the initial solution sought to streamline our enrollment process, and we have successfully moved from a nine-step wizard to a one-step wizard. After talking to subject matter experts, I learned that the most important steps for users to interact with were in the last few steps where we were seeing less than 20% interaction. The original wizard was lengthy in order to prove users with a sense of customization but failed to actually encourage customization. Enrollment rates have increased by 60%. 20% of users are willingly engaging in customization.
