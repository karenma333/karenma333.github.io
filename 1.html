Advice Proposal PDF

How can we summarize our service into one document?

Summary

At any point in a user's interaction with the managed accounts platform, they can view a pdf snapshot of their account. The wide variety of user states and scenarios means this document needs to be multi-functional without being overwhelming with information.

  • Context: Team of 3 Designers
  • Role: Lead Designer, Qualitative Researcher
  • Tools: Requirements Gathering, Interviews, Usability Testing, Parallel Design Prototyping, Card Sort

Evaluating the Foundation

Using the existing version of this document as a basis, I created cards to represent the pieces of information to perform a hybrid card sort. This allowed me to see what information users were still interested in seeing and what gaps of information the current document did not fill. Below, you’ll see the existing version of this document with some notes overlaid.

Divergent Designs

The card sort results combined with internal stakeholder needs informed the content of this document. From there, we mocked-up multiple visuals of the PDF. Using these visuals we performed a series of usability tests as well as A/B preference tests to help us converge into our final design.

Final Design

  1. 1. Removal of Current vs. Proposed Concept
    Emphaiss on the advice and recommendations provided.
  2. 2. Emphasis of retirement readiness metric
    While already on the first page, we made it take more space to give it the same importance users were giving it
  3. 3. Improved Contribution Details
    As this is one of the few things a user has control over, we gave more details to allow users to maximize their potential
  4. 4. Deemphasis of Portfolio
    Since the goal of the service is managed advice and adjusting your portfolio over time, we've removed the callouts to the Portfolio.
