Balancing Customization with Efficiency

Team size: 1
Role: Sole designer
Skillset: User analytics

product opportunity

After launching our first user-facing product, the analytics data for enrollment showed a drop-off rate during the initial welcome flow.

design overview

I was the sole designer on this project and utilized a combination of web analytics data, qualitative surveys and market research to provide design insights.

initial insights

I was the sole designer on this project and utilized a combination of web analytics data, qualitative surveys and market research to provide design insights.

Users are not finishing the enrollment process.

Over 60% of users are dropping off, users are disinclined to continue and give up because they have no insight on how many steps are in our enrollment process.

Users are not using our wizard as desired.

On the last three steps, users are clicking ‘Next’ without interacting with the application. Users are ignoring and not recognizing our request for personalization.

redefined problem statement

How can we improve the way we personalize the experience?

design highlights

In this redesign, we also reviewed the copy and standardized key terminology as well as consistency in number formatting, such as rounding and input validation.

Simplified Introductory Wizard.

Since the wizard isn’t being used, I condensed the wizard into a single page.

Highlighted Key Personalization.

While the more information we know is better, I determined the most important and easiest pieces of personalization to request.

next steps

While the initial solution sought to streamline our enrollment process, and we have successfully moved from a nine-step wizard to a one-step wizard. After talking to subject matter experts, I learned that the most important steps for users to interact with were in the last few steps where we were seeing less than 20% interaction. The original wizard was lengthy in order to prove users with a sense of personalization but failed to actually encourage personalization. How can we improve and highlight the need for users to provide important information?
